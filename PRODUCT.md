# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Ashley.dev serves a balanced mix of developers, Developer Relations peers, open-source community members, event organizers, and potential collaborators. They arrive with limited time and need to understand Ashley's credibility, values, perspective, and body of work without navigating a conventional resume or corporate biography.

## Product Purpose

Ashley.dev is Ashley Willis's personal portfolio and publishing home. It brings together her leadership experience, open-source work, writing, projects, and speaking so visitors leave with a clear sense of both her technical credibility and her human-centered approach to developer communities. Success means visitors can quickly understand what Ashley stands for, find relevant work or ideas, and confidently choose a next step such as reading, inviting her to speak, or getting in touch.

## Brand Personality

Candid, humane, and technically credible. The voice should be direct and specific without becoming cold, self-serious, or overly polished. It should pair earned authority with curiosity, warmth, and an honest respect for the people behind technical systems.

## Anti-references

The terminal and IDE structure is an intentional part of Ashley.dev's identity. Avoid generic implementations of that idea: noisy boot sequences, commands that reveal nothing useful, illegible code decoration, or technical styling disconnected from Ashley's real work. The site should also avoid generic SaaS landing-page patterns, inflated self-promotion, and corporate executive-bio language.

## Design Principles

1. **Put people before platforms.** Connect technical work to the people, communities, and outcomes it serves.
2. **Show evidence instead of asserting expertise.** Let concrete projects, talks, writing, and experience establish credibility.
3. **Make the terminal language earn its place.** Commands, filenames, and code-shaped content should reveal something true about Ashley or help visitors navigate.
4. **Make the next meaningful path obvious.** Help visitors move naturally from Ashley's perspective to her writing, work, speaking, or contact details.
5. **Let candor build trust.** Prefer clear, grounded language and honest perspective over polish for its own sake.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. Future work must preserve strong color contrast, complete keyboard access, visible focus states, semantic structure, and robust screen-reader labeling. Motion must respect `prefers-reduced-motion`, and interactions must not rely on color, hover, or animation alone.
